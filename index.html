About

I am senior researcher and machine learning scientist at CERN with a PhD in physics. For the past ten years I have been analyzing data from the CMS experiment at the CERN laboratory in Geneva, one the most complex physics experiments ever built, with a delivered data flow of terabytes per second. Leveraging the power of ML and advanced statistics, I have been pushing science forward, answering questions about the fundamental laws of Nature.

I have 10 years of experience in analyzing large-scale heterogeneous datasets, machine learning engineering, and software development. I hold a PhD in Physics from ETH Zurich, MSc in High Energy Physics from Ecole Polytechnique Paris and ETH Zurich, and BSc in Applied Mathematics and Physics from MIPT.

My job is to translate problems into scalable solutions.

Work

Motivated to contribute to the collective knowledge of humanity, I leverage technology to unlock the secrets of the universe. Grounded in fundamental physics, my research seeks answers to intriguing questions about the origin and the fate of the universe. By applying AI and big data techniques, I analyze the aftermath of the high-energy proton collisions at CERN’s Large Hadron Collider (LHC) to learn about how the universe evolved just moments after the Big Bang.

My recent research has revolved around bringing state-of-the-art ML techniques to the scientific community. I have been working on and led projects dedicated to integrating deep learning in key stages of the workflow at the LHC, such as real-time ML inference, efficient pattern recognition, synthetic data generation, and anomaly detection. These new techniques allow scientists like myself to extend the discovery reach, and tackle unprecedented challenges that experimental particle physics will face in the next decade.

My research in fundamental physics delivered the world's most stringent constraints on double-Higgs production's cross section which helps scientists to shed light on our understanding of the early evolution of the universe and its eventual fate. I also developed a new algorithm that improved the reconstruction of one of the most complex collision products at the LHC. The improvement brought by this algorithm helped to accomplish the milestone observation of the Higgs boson decay to bottom quarks. In turn, this seminal achievement, elucidated one of the key missing pieces of particle physics theory - originally considered unreachable for many years to come.

I have been invited to give talks and seminars on ML and particle physics at prestigious universities and international conferences around the world.
